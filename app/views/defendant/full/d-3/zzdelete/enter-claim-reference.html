What happens once I've viewed the claim?

If you accept the claim is fair you can respond to say that you agree to pay the money. If you can't afford to pay it all at once you can make a suggestion, such as a repayment plan, for them to consider.

If you don't accept you owe the amount of money claimed you'll be asked to submit your defence and might have to go to a hearing. You'll also have the option to make a 'without prejudice' suggestion to try to settle the claim. This means the other side can’t mention that you made a suggestion to them if they choose to reject it and you do end up at a hearing.

If you believe the claimant owes you money you can make a claim against them (‘counterclaim’). You might have to pay a court fee.
