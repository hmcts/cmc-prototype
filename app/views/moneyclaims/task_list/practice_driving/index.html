Rules for practicing driving

Practice driving

To practise driving there are rules, rights and responsibilities:

You must:

  • drive in a safe car
  • drive in a insured car
  • drive in a taxed car
  • have a provisional licence
  • practise with a driving instructor or fully licenced driver
  • ensure the people you practise with are insured on the car you practise in

You can't:

  • practise on the motorway

You can:

  • practise on dual carriageway
  • practise driving at night
  • take your driving test in your own car
  • take your driving test without having had lessons
  • practise without a driving instructor, as long as you have a fully licenced driver with you, who is 25 or older and insured on the same car
  • drive with other people in your car, as long as you have a fully licenced driver with you, who is 25 or older and insured on the same car
