Jan Clark's claim

Jan Clark has made a money claim against you.

You have until 7 June to respond to this claim.

Claim amount: £1,500

Reason for claim

Mary Richards and I signed a contract for building works on my roof. The work on the roof was completed but there was a problem with one section. I asked Mary Richards to get the problem fixed, but didn't get a reply.

I'll have to get another builder to rectify the problem which will cost me £1,500.

Therefore, I'm making a money claim for £1,500 to compensate for the extra money I'm going to need to spend.

You have until 7 June to respond to this claim.

What if I don't respond in time?

You might have to pay more or get a County Court Judgment (CCJ) if you don't respond in time.

What ways can I respond?

If you accept the claim is fair you can respond to say that you agree to pay the money. If you can't afford to pay it all at once you can make a suggestion, such as a repayment plan, for them to consider.

If you don't accept you owe the amount of money claimed you'll be asked to submit your defence and might have to go to a hearing. You'll also have the option to make a 'without prejudice' suggestion to try to settle the claim. This means the other side can’t mention that you made them an offer if they choose to reject it and you do end up at a hearing.

If you believe the claimant owes you money you can make a claim against them (‘counterclaim’). You might have to pay a court fee.

Other questions

Can I just pay the money now?

Yes, you can contact Jan Clark yourself and pay the money. Ask them to let the court know when they have your payment.

What if they owe me money?

You can make a claim against them (‘counterclaim’) if you think they owe you money. Download and fill in form N9B.

What if I can't afford to pay the money back?

You might be able to get expert advice to help you deal with debt from organisations like StepChange.

You can also respond to the claim and offer a repayment plan.
