Terms and conditions

This page explains the Civil Money Claims service’s terms of use. They include this website’s [privacy policy](https://www.gov.uk/help/privacy-policy) and [terms and conditions](https://www.gov.uk/help/terms-conditions). You must agree to these to use this service.

Who we are

The Civil Money Claims service is managed by Her Majesty’s Court and Tribunal Service and will be referred to as ‘we’ from now on.

We may update these terms and conditions if there is a change in the law or to the way the service works.

Information provided by this service

This service provides information to support your application for probate. We can’t give legal advice on individual cases.

You should answer the questions in the service based on your circumstances, and seek legal advice if you need it.

Where your data is stored

We use Amazon Web Services (AWS) to store your data, on secure servers within the European Economic Area (EEA). By submitting your data, you consent to the AWS Terms and Conditions.

We use GOV.UK Notify to send emails. Until the point where emails are handed over to your email provider, they are processed within the EEA. Your data is stored for 1 month, and is deleted after this.

The service is intended for use by claimants and defendants or by others on their behalf and only with their consent.

There are risks in using a shared computer (for example in an internet café) to access this service. It’s your responsibility to be aware of these risks and to avoid using any computer which may leave your personal information accessible to others. You’re responsible if you choose to leave a computer unprotected while using this service.

malicious computer code other forms of interference which may damage your computer system You must not misuse this service by knowingly introducing viruses, trojans, worms, logic bombs or other material which is malicious or technologically harmful.
