Statement of truth

{% if data['claimant-type']=="Company" or data['claimant-type']=="Organisation" or data['check']==1%}

You must hold a senior position in your company to sign the statement of truth.

Types of senior position

A senior position could be a:

  • director
  • treasurer
  • company secretary
  • chief executive
  • manager or other officer of the company or corporation

If your company isn't registered, a senior position could also be the:

  • mayor
  • chairman
  • president
  • town clerk or other similar officer of the corporation.

If your company is a partnership, a senior position is held by:

  • any of the partners
  • a person who controls or manages the partnership business

Insurers

A senior insurer or the Motor Insurers’ Bureau can sign this if they have a financial interest in the result of the claim against the defendant.

Managing agents

Managing agents cannot sign the statement of truth. In this case it must be signed by either the claimant or their legal representative.

Trusts

If some or all of the trustees comprise a single party, one, or all of the trustees can sign the statement of truth.

Authorised person

If you can’t read or sign the claim, the statement of truth must be signed by an authorised person.

An authorised person is someone who can administer oaths and take affidavits, such as a solicitor. They must certify that:<[/p>]

  • document has been read by the claimant
  • the claimant understood and approved the claim
  • they read the statement of truth to the claimant
  • the claimant understood the statement of truth and the consequences of making a false statement
  • the claimant agreed (‘signed or made their mark’) to the statement in their presence
