Prove your version of the dispute

As the defendant has disputed your claim, you need to produce a statement that uses facts and evidence to help convince the judge that your version of events is the true one.

This is also known as your 'witness statement'.

A good witness statement:

  • states facts, not opinions
  • clearly reference supporting evidence or sources of facts
  • follows a chronological order of events
  • is clear, concise and to the point
  • covers all the differences between your view of the case and that of the other party
  • avoids or explains jargon
  • answers any questions a judge might have about your case
  • is truthful and not misleading

In order to produce your witness statement, we will ask you:

  • for any evidence you want to reference in your statement
  • how this evidence and any other facts support your version of the claim
  • how this evidence and any other facts dissprove the defendant's version of the claim
  • for any additional information
