Apply to set aside the CCJ

Setting aside a CCJ is usually only possible if you have a good reason. For example, you did not receive, or did not respond to, the original claim from the court saying you owed the money.

You’ll have to go to a private hearing at the court to explain why you do not owe the money.

If your application is successful

Your CCJ will be removed from public records. In most cases you'll still have to respond to the claim.

You can request the claimant refund your £255 fee.

If your application is unsuccessful

You might have to pay the claimant's costs. For example their legal fees or travel costs if it goes to a hearing.

The CCJ will stay on the register for 6 years.
